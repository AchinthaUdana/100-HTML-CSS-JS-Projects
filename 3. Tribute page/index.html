Nikola Tesla

The Man Who Lit the World

The Genius Ahead of His Time

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system. His innovations in electromagnetism laid the foundation for wireless communication and energy.

Tesla held over 300 patents worldwide and his inventions include the Tesla coil, induction motor, and wireless transmission of energy. Despite his brilliance, he died in relative obscurity, but his legacy continues to inspire scientists and engineers today.

Key Events in Tesla's Life

1856

Born on July 10 in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).

1875

Enrolled at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz to study electrical engineering.

1882

Discovered the rotating magnetic field, the foundation of alternating current (AC) machinery.

1884

Immigrated to the United States and began working for Thomas Edison.

1888

Patented the AC induction motor and transformer, licensed by George Westinghouse.

1891

Invented the Tesla coil, a high-voltage transformer used in radio technology.

1893

Demonstrated wireless power transmission and illuminated the Chicago World's Fair with AC power.

1943

Died on January 7 in New York City at age 86.
